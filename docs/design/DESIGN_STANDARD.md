- `docs/design/reference/standard-character-space-01.png`
- `docs/design/reference/standard-character-space-02.png`

## Target direction

Agent Mission Control should feel like a warm, tactile, miniature operations room inhabited by modern humanoid figurines.

### Character standard

- Modern miniature human figurines, not mascots or creatures.
- Soft clay/plastic-like physicality: rounded forms, matte dimensional shading, gentle shadows.
- Simplified but human proportions: recognizable heads, torsos, limbs, hair/outfit cues.
- Subtle agent identity through posture, outfit, accessories, and work/rest behavior.
- Avoid emoji, anime, pixel art, Pokémon-like character design, Slack-style avatars, or flat icon people.

### Workspace standard

- A purposeful miniature operations-room/diorama, not a decorative hill/planet/blob.
- Required zones:
  - work console / status wall
  - mission/task board
  - provider/server alcove
  - walkway or navigable floor plane
  - chill/nap nook for idle agents
- Characters should inhabit the room naturally: working, checking the board, walking, chilling, or napping.
- The home page should read as a living mission-control dashboard first; dense admin/config controls belong in sidebar routes or drawers.

### Clock standard

- Use a real-time digital wall clock synced to browser/system time.
- Avoid analog or decorative clocks unless they serve a clear visual purpose.
